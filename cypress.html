Cypress Point Club

Cypress Point is located on the Monterey Peninsula in California. Designed by Alister MacKenzie, it is known for its dramatic coastal setting, with holes that play through dunes, forest, and along cliffs overlooking the Pacific Ocean.

The course feels natural, with holes that follow the land rather than feeling artificially made. The routing takes players through different environments, building toward a famous stretch along the shoreline on the back nine. In terms of course difficulty, it is rated at a 73.1/141.

Signature Stretch

The closing holes on the back nine are some of the most photographed in golf. One par 3 plays across a rocky cove to a green set over the ocean, capturing the challenge and beauty of coastal golf. Hole 16 is a 233 yard par 3 that boasts a challenge to golfers of all levels, even the most elite. With a force carry of over 200 yards, a poor shot is almost an automatic bogey.
